# Technical Note for the RTFO Administrator / UK Department for Transport (DfT)

**Subject:** Description of the Chain-of-Custody Architecture and Traceability System Governing End-of-Life Tyres (ELT), Certified Products, and Process Co-Products

## Preamble and Scope

This technical note sets out, in comprehensive and granular detail, the operational architecture, documentary substrate, and quantitative-verification logic of the traceability and chain-of-custody system applied to the End-of-Life Tyres (ELT) feedstock stream and its derived product fractions, as implemented within the framework of the ISCC certification system (ISCC EU and ISCC PLUS) and for the express purpose of supporting demonstrable conformity with the Renewable Transport Fuel Obligation (RTFO) and the associated verification expectations of the United Kingdom Department for Transport.

The system has been designed to permit the uninterrupted reconstruction of the complete chain of custody — from the point of origin of the material at the collecting point through to final delivery to the end customer in the United Kingdom — with full, on-demand availability of all underlying supporting documentation, and with the capability to extract, upon request and at any node of the supply chain, the relevant contracts, Proofs of Sustainability (PoS), transport documents, and any further audit-grade evidentiary material. The system additionally enables independent verification of daily ELT intake loads, of produced quantities expressed both in kilograms (mass) and in litres (volume) by means of density-variable conversion tables, and of the biogenic-matrix value attributed to the product by accredited laboratory determination via Carbon-14 (C14) radiocarbon analysis and correlated test methods.

## 1. Feedstock Reception at the Collecting Point

The material flow originates at the **collecting point**, where ELT consignments are received against an **electronic eRSV** (electronic receipt of material) issued by the supplier, the **PoS** associated with the specific batch/lot, the **daily intake registration**, and the corresponding **weighbridge evidence and logistical traceability records**. This documentary set permits the unambiguous identification of the incoming material, its associated lot reference, the delivered quantity, and the explicit linkage of each consignment to the ISCC system, thereby establishing the first verifiable node of the chain of custody and the point of entry of sustainability characteristics into the mass-balance accounting boundary.

## 2. OISTEBIO Girardot — Reception and Processing

The material is subsequently transferred to the **OISTEBIO Girardot** facility, where it is received as **certified feedstock** and incorporated into the certified-mass management system. Each lot is registered with date and time of arrival, gross weight, tare, net discharged weight, the reference of the governing transport document, and the reference of the linked PoS. The facility performs thermochemical conversion by means of **pyrolysis**, generating multiple output streams that are either certified or otherwise registered within the facility's mass-balance management system, with each stream allocated against the certified input pool in accordance with the applicable ISCC mass-balance methodology.

## 3. Process Outputs

The production process yields the following streams:

- **Certified EU light fraction**, designated internally as **DEV-P100**, representing approximately 30% of the total oil output;
- **Certified PLUS heavy fraction**, designated internally as **DEV-P200**;
- **Further co-products and process streams**, including carbon black, syngas, process water, and metal scrap.

All such outputs are recorded in the facility management system so as to guarantee full reconciliation between input, production yield, and aggregate output, preserving mass continuity across the conversion boundary and enabling the quantitative closure of the mass balance at any selected accounting interval.

## 4. Quantitative Control and Conversions

The system permits verification of: the daily volumes of ELT received; production expressed in both kilograms and litres; the conversion between mass and volume by means of **density-variable conversion tables**; and the coherence between weighbridge records, production figures, and the quantities declared in the sustainability documentation. The system further permits the association, with the product, of the **biogenic-matrix value** resulting from laboratory analysis, including **C14 radiocarbon determinations** where applicable, thereby substantiating the renewable/biogenic content claim that underpins eligibility considerations.

## 5. Export Documentation — Colombia → Netherlands → United Kingdom

The export segment of the flow is supported by a complete documentary set:

- **Departure from Colombia:** the flow is accompanied by the port RSV, the **Bill of Lading**, and the PoS associated with the exported consignment.
- **Logistical transfer in the Netherlands:** a **stop-and-go** operation at **UTB BV, Dordrecht**, with optional **ISO-to-ISO repacking** and uninterrupted preservation of chain-of-custody continuity.
- **Final movement to the United Kingdom:** supported by the **Bill of Lading from the Netherlands to the United Kingdom** and the **commercial invoice to Crown Oil Ltd**, together with the batch- and product-level supporting documentation for the certified product.

## 6. Audit and Traceability Functionality

The system enables the end-to-end reconstruction of the entire chain of custody and the on-demand extraction of all documents material to audit or verification, including: contracts, PoS, eRSV / RSV, Bills of Lading, commercial invoices, weighbridge registers, production registers, and warehouse / mass-reconciliation records. This permits verification, at any point in time, of the coherence between incoming material, processed quantity, produced quantity, the EU- and PLUS-certified streams, the co-products, the export shipments, and the final delivery to the UK customer.

## 7. Conclusion

The system is structured to guarantee complete and verifiable traceability of the entire ELT flow — from collection through transformation, through the logistical transfer in the Netherlands, and to final delivery in the United Kingdom. The combination of electronic documents, PoS, weighbridge records, densimetric conversions, process registers, and laboratory analyses sustains a robust chain of custody, conformant with the audit requirements of the RTFO / UK DfT framework.
