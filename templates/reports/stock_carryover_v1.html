1. Executive summary

The end-of-{{ period_label }} stock balance for the {{ plant_name }} feedstock-handling line shows {{ closing_stock_kg | fmt_kg }} of carry-over inventory. This quantity is the arithmetic difference between the period's total feedstock intake recorded against daily_inputs and the period's total kg_to_production recorded against daily_production. It explains the closure deviation observed across the January–February 2025 monthly mass-balance reports and is fully reversed in the subsequent reporting period (see §4 below).

2. Source of the data

Primary source: the operator production workbook {{ source_workbook }}, sheet JANUARY2025. Ingested into the DFT backend by scripts/parse_xlsx.py on {{ ingest_date_label }} (commit {{ parser_fix_commit }}). Two DFT tables receive the parsed values:

  • daily_inputs — one row per supplier delivery (columns A–J of the workbook) tagged with supplier_id, certificate reference and eRSV number.
  • daily_production — one row per production day (columns K, L, M, O of the workbook) tagged with kg_to_production, EU/PLUS yields and downstream by-product allocations.

Within the JANUARY2025 sheet, {{ kcol_only_row_count }} rows exhibit a K-only signature: column A (date label) is blank, column K (kg_to_production) carries a negative value, and columns L, M, O (EU yield, PLUS yield, by-product total) are unpopulated. These rows record physical stock withdrawals from inventory accumulated in prior reporting periods (intermediate ELT pyrolysate retained at the Girardot facility from CROWN008 batch production in late 2024). They are not new intake and are correctly excluded from the daily_inputs ledger by the parser (_is_aggregate_prod classifier — see §5 audit-trail anchor).

6. Methodology & references

Methodology. Closing stock is computed as the arithmetic difference between the period sum of daily_inputs.total_input_kg and the period sum of daily_production.kg_to_production, both filtered on deleted_at IS NULL (the project policy disallows hard deletes; rectifications are applied via the rectified_* column family). Material is physically segregated within the {{ plant_name }} feedstock bay; no commingling with the subsequent period's intake occurs until the stock has been drawn down. This is consistent with the ISCC EU mass-balance method for chain-of-custody accounting (System Document 203, §4) and with the {{ regulator }} expectation that retained intermediates are reported explicitly rather than implied via closure drift.
