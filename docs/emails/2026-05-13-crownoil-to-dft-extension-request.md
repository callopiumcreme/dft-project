Further to the telephone conversation earlier today, Crown Oil is writing to formally request an extension of the 14 May 2026 ROS submission deadline in respect of the **January 2025 bundle (RTFO-310125)** under the RTFO 2025 End-of-Life Tyres pathway.

**1. Request**

We respectfully request that the deadline for submission of RTFO-310125 be extended from 14 May 2026 to **13 June 2026** — a thirty-day extension applying solely to the January 2025 bundle. We further commit to providing a **written interim status report on 30 May 2026** against the Evidence Register accompanying this letter, so that DfT may monitor progress against the milestones we set out below.

**2. Scope discipline**

We have given careful consideration to the feedback in DfT's previous correspondence regarding "incremental and inconsistent submission" of evidence. In response, the present request is deliberately limited to a single calendar month. We are not requesting an extension for the February–August 2025 bundles at this time. Subsequent 2025 bundles will be brought forward for separate consideration only after RTFO-310125 has been accepted, in a single coherent submission for each, and in dialogue with the Unit on appropriate timing. The intent is to demonstrate, through this submission, the standard at which any subsequent submission would be prepared.

**3. Evidence accompanying this request**

To demonstrate that the requested extension will be used to deliver an audit-grade submission rather than to defer obligations, we attach an initial body of evidence:

- **Annex A — Reconciled daily mass-balance, January 2025.** Generated from the OisteBio digital ingest system. Includes per-day detail, monthly aggregate, and explicit reconciliation of end-January stock movement. PDF, signed, with SHA-256 hash for integrity verification.
- **Annex D — Stock carry-over end-January 2025.** Written explanation of the 339.865 kg physical feedstock retained in inventory at end-January 2025, documented per source record. This accounts for the apparent January closure variance and confirms the inventory is physically present in the OisteBio facility, traceable in internal records, and not lost or under-declared.
- **Annex E — Milestone plan.** Tabular summary of the work programme between today and 13 June 2026, identifying owners and target completion dates for each evidence item required for the final RTFO-310125 submission.

Two further annexes will be transmitted within 24 hours as supplementary submission:

- **Annex B — Supply chain diagram.** Four-layer diagram (origin point where applicable, collecting point with ISCC certification, OisteBio facility, Crown Oil) post-remediation of supplier classification.
- **Annex C — Evidence Register.** Full register of documents required for the January 2025 resubmission with current status, owner, and target completion date. This register will form the basis of the 30 May 2026 interim status report.

**4. Work programme to 13 June 2026**

Between today and the requested deadline, the following workstreams will be executed by OisteBio and Crown Oil:

- Retroactive Proof-of-Sustainability (ISCC) from each collecting point (Litoplas, Biowaste, Esenttia) for January 2025 lots only, secured through written request to each compliance lead within the first week.
- Surgical supplier reclassification limited to January 2025 daily inputs, applied via the OisteBio digital ingest system with full audit trail (no hard delete, no silent rewrites; volume totals preserved, every reattribution logged with old and new values).
- ANLA / Ministerio de Ambiente regulatory documentation for each collecting point.
- Consular legalisation (UK Consulate Bogotá) and sworn EN translations of Colombian documents pertaining to January 2025.
- Final internal audit cross-referencing every kilogram of January 2025 input to its PoS ISCC and provider registration record, followed by submission on ROS no later than 13 June 2026.

**5. Why this request, and why now**

The two months since DfT's last correspondence have been used to construct the digital infrastructure (audit log, cryptographic export, collecting-point schema, production litres schema, mass-balance reconciliation engine) necessary to deliver evidence at the standard the Unit has indicated it expects. That infrastructure is now in place; what remains is the assembly of the third-party supplier documentation, which is unavoidably gated on counterparty response times.

We acknowledge that the extension is sought close to the deadline, and we appreciate that we are asking for the Unit's flexibility on a compressed timeline. We make this request because we believe the alternative — submission of an incomplete bundle on 14 May 2026 — would fail the same tests as the first investigation and serve neither the Unit's interest nor ours. We would rather submit one disciplined bundle thirty days later than submit a deficient bundle on time.
