# RTFO — Renewable Transport Fuel Obligation (Essential Guide)

> Source: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/about-the-rtfo/the-rtfo-an-essential-guide
> Fetched: 2026-05-12
> Authority: UK Department for Transport (DfT) — Low Carbon Fuels (LCF) Delivery Unit
> Contact: rtfo-compliance@dft.gov.uk

---

## 1. Overview

The Renewable Transport Fuel Obligation (RTFO) is the UK scheme that obliges suppliers of fossil and renewable transport fuel to demonstrate that a defined share of their supply is sustainable renewable fuel. It commenced on **15 April 2008** and is one of the UK government's main policies for reducing GHG emissions from transport.

Compliance is evidenced through tradeable certificates — **Renewable Transport Fuel Certificates (RTFCs)** — awarded per litre (or equivalent) of sustainable renewable fuel supplied. Suppliers either redeem enough RTFCs or pay a fixed buy-out per missing certificate.

### 2023 performance
- 3.7 billion litres of renewable fuel supplied
- 7.5% of total fuel for road + non-road mobile machinery
- 7.9 Mt CO2eq GHG savings delivered

---

## 2. Scope — who is obligated

### Registration threshold

A supplier must register with the LCF Delivery Unit if they supply **≥ 450,000 litres** of relevant fuel for use in a relevant transport mode during an obligation period.

### Relevant fuels
- Petrol
- Diesel
- Gas oil
- Renewable fuel

### Relevant transport modes
- Road vehicles
- Non-road transports (including non-road mobile machinery)
- Maritime (only if fuel is a Renewable Fuel of Non-Biological Origin — RFNBO)

---

## 3. Obligation structure

### Two-element targets (2025 values)

| Component | 2025 target |
|---|---|
| Main obligation | **14.054%** |
| Development fuel sub-target | **1.619%** |

### Obligation calculation

`Obligation amount = Obligated amount × Obligation %`

where **Obligated amount** = total fossil + unsustainable renewable fuel supplied (i.e. total fuel supplied minus sustainable renewable fuel).

---

## 4. Compliance mechanisms

Obligated suppliers meet their obligation by either:

1. **Redeeming RTFCs** (own-generated or purchased on open market)
2. **Buy-out payment** — fixed sum per missing certificate

### Buy-out prices (current)

| Type | Buy-out |
|---|---|
| Standard RTFC | **£0.50 / certificate** |
| Development RTFC (dRTFC) | **£0.80 / certificate** |

---

## 5. RTFCs — Renewable Transport Fuel Certificates

Awarded per litre (or equivalent) of sustainable renewable fuel supplied. Three certificate classes:

| Class | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Relevant crop RTFC | Crop-derived fuels — subject to the **crop cap** |
| Development RTFC (dRTFC) | Counts toward the development fuel sub-target |
| General RTFC | All other sustainable renewable fuels |

### Double-counting

Some feedstocks earn **2× RTFCs per litre**:
- Certain wastes and residues
- Dedicated energy crops
- RFNBOs

### Single dRTFC

- **Recycled Carbon Fuels (RCFs)** are awarded **1 dRTFC per litre** supplied (no doubling).

---

## 6. Fuel categories

### Renewable Fuels of Non-Biological Origin (RFNBOs)

Produced from non-biomass renewable sources — solar, wind, geothermal, hydro. Simplest form: hydrogen from electrolysis powered by renewable electricity.

Specific GHG methodology + **additionality** rules apply for grid-electricity-based RFNBOs (see separate RFNBO guidance document).

### Recycled Carbon Fuels (RCFs)

Fuels made from a **fossil waste** that cannot be recycled, reused or prevented, AND that has been designated as a relevant feedstock by the LCF Delivery Unit. RCFs use a **counterfactual GHG methodology** — see Annex D of the RTFO and SAF Mandate technical guidance.

### Partially renewable / co-processed fuels

- **Partially renewable:** mixed fossil + renewable inputs
- **Co-processed:** fossil and renewable intermediates processed together in the same refinery

The renewable share of output = **% by energy of feedstocks that are renewable**.

### Biomethane and gases

Same provisions as liquid renewable fuels with these specifics:
- Reported in **kg** (not litres)
- Multipliers applied per gas for RTFC award
- Biomethane suppliers must follow specific Biomethane guidance

---

## 7. Development fuels (sub-target detail)

To qualify for dRTFCs, a fuel must be:
- An RFNBO, or
- An RCF, or
- Made from a feedstock designated by the LCF Delivery Unit as a **double-counting waste that is not a segregated oil and fat**

### Eligible development fuel types
1. Hydrogen
2. Substitute natural gas (renewable methane from gasification or pyrolysis)
3. Drop-in petrol or diesel **blendable to ≥ 25%** while meeting the relevant fuel standards

### Award rates
- All development fuels: **2× dRTFCs per litre** equivalent
- **Exception:** RCFs receive **1 dRTFC per litre** equivalent

---

## 8. Sustainability & carbon criteria

### GHG saving threshold

Renewable fuels must deliver **55% to 65% GHG savings vs fossil counterfactual** (exact threshold depends on when the production installation started operating).

For **RCFs** the GHG methodology is distinct (Annex D — counterfactual methodology).

### Land criteria
Preserves land with high biodiversity value or high carbon stock from being converted for fuel-feedstock production.

### Forest criteria (where forest biomass is used)
- Legal harvesting
- Forest regeneration
- Maintenance of soil carbon

### Soil carbon criteria (agricultural wastes/residues)
Impacts on soil quality and soil carbon from removing agricultural wastes/residues must be **monitored and addressed**.

---

## 9. Compliance cycle — four steps

| Step | What happens |
|---|---|
| 1. Fuel volumes | Supplier reports volumes via RTFO Operating System (**ROS**), validated against **HMRC data** |
| 2. RTFC applications | Submit certificate applications — independently verified + LCF targeted checks before award |
| 3. Trading | Certificates trade on open market — purely-renewable suppliers can sell to fossil suppliers short of compliance |
| 4. Meet obligation | By **15 September** of year following obligation period, redeem enough RTFCs or pay buy-out |

### Period dates
- **Obligation period:** 1 January → 31 December
- **Compliance deadline:** 15 September of the following year

---

## 10. Verification

All RTFC applications must be **independently verified** and must demonstrate compliance with sustainability criteria. The RTFO Third-Party Assurance Guidance covers the role of **verifiers and voluntary schemes**.

> Note — the essential guide does not name ISCC EU explicitly, but voluntary schemes are referenced as a recognised assurance route via the Third-Party Assurance Guidance. ISCC EU is one of the schemes recognised by DfT in practice (cross-check Third-Party Assurance Guidance for current list).

---

## 11. Carry-over of RTFCs

- **Unused RTFCs** from an obligation period can be carried over **once** (one year forward only).
- Example: 2024 RTFCs usable in 2025, **not** 2026.
- **Cap:** up to **25%** of an obligation can be met using carried-over certificates from the previous period.

---

## 12. Alternative assessment points (fuels without HMRC duty point)

| Fuel | Assessment point |
|---|---|
| Renewable hydrogen | Point of sale for consumption in transport |
| Maritime RFNBOs | Point at which dispensed for ship use |
| Renewable gases in non-road transports | Point at which fuel is "set aside" for non-road use |

For these fuels HMRC reconciliation is not possible — LCF Delivery Unit requests additional evidence (e.g. sales invoices) to prove use in a relevant UK transport mode.

## 13. Feedstocks

### Pre-approved
A list of pre-assessed and approved feedstocks is published online (see *List of Feedstocks*).

### New feedstocks
Suppliers contact LCF Delivery Unit → feedstock assessment determines classification.

### New production processes
Engage with LCF Delivery Unit. They may issue a **provisional assessment** of RTFC eligibility.

## 14. Non-compliance

| Consequence | Trigger |
|---|---|
| RTFC revocation | Certificates awarded on erroneous application |
| Civil penalties | Serious non-compliance — see Chapter 6 of RTFO Compliance Guidance |

## 16. Glossary (quick reference)

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| RTFO | Renewable Transport Fuel Obligation |
| RTFC | Renewable Transport Fuel Certificate (1 per litre / equivalent) |
| dRTFC | Development fuel RTFC |
| LCF Delivery Unit | DfT body that runs the scheme |
| ROS | RTFO Operating System (supplier portal) |
| RFNBO | Renewable Fuel of Non-Biological Origin |
| RCF | Recycled Carbon Fuel |
| Obligated amount | Total fossil + unsustainable renewable fuel supplied (basis for % calculation) |
| Crop cap | Limit on volume of crop-derived RTFCs counting toward obligation |
| Buy-out | Fixed per-certificate payment in lieu of RTFC redemption |
