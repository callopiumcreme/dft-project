Methodology & References

Volumetric conversion applies the canonical relation litres = kg / density at constant product density measured at 15 °C. The two nominal densities used in this report are:

  • EU-grade ({{ product_eu_code }}) — ρEU = {{ density_eu | fmt_density }}
  • PLUS-grade ({{ product_plus_code }}) — ρPLUS = {{ density_plus | fmt_density }}

Both density values are ISCC-recognised product-specification figures for the OisteBio {{ product }} family and are loaded into the platform product_densities seed table by Alembic migration 0005_seed_product_densities.py. They are immutable for the duration of a reporting period and are re-validated against laboratory analysis at every product release.

Per-row litre values shown in the daily table are not stored independently — they are derived deterministically inside PostgreSQL as GENERATED ALWAYS columns on the daily_production table: 

litres_eu   NUMERIC GENERATED ALWAYS AS (eu_prod_kg   / {{ density_eu }})  STORED,
litres_plus NUMERIC GENERATED ALWAYS AS (plus_prod_kg / {{ density_plus }}) STORED

These columns are introduced by Alembic migration 0007_persist_production_litres.py. Once persisted, historical litre values become immutable for audit purposes even if product densities are revised in the future. Write access to litres_eu and litres_plus is prohibited at the schema level: any attempt to update them raises PostgreSQL error code 42601.
